Activated Carbon Methane Mitigation

This awesome Solution designed by 23201 group

In 2013, Beijing faced a severe air pollution crisis known as the "Airpocalypse," with dangerously high levels of fine particulate matter. This crisis highlighted the urgent need for air treatment. Visibility dropped to near zero, and the air was so toxic that people struggled to breathe. So, the problem the project attempts to solve is using polluted air from industrial waste and converting it into clean air. The prototype’s target is to get clean air for breathing from the input air which is polluted by “Methane.” This prototype was made with three phases, the first one is to medical gauze that captures large particles, then passes through activated carbon, and then to fine filter to ensure that all impurities are gone completely. This project has the potential to successfully meet the design requirements. When the prototype has been evaluated, the prototype has a purification percentage of about 25% of the input air. Finally, this project would have an efficiency of more than 25%.

Test pLan

"Testing begins: will the prototype deliver its promise?“

Volume

To ensure that the air sample meets the required range (600–1500 mL), the volume will be calculated indirectly using the density formula as the density of methane gas is constant and measuring the mass of the sample using a sensitive balance.

Time

The time required to process the air sample within the system will be measured to ensure the process is completed in less than 10 minutes.

concentration

The concentration of air pollutants (methane and volatile organic compounds) will be measured to ensure the system effectively reduces pollutants by at least 20%. The concentration will be calculated directly using the MQ-5 sensors.
